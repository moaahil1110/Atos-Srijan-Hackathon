HIPAA Security Rule — Encryption (Transmission Security)
45 CFR §164.312(e)(2)(ii)

Standard: Transmission Security
A covered entity or business associate must implement technical security measures to
guard against unauthorized access to ePHI that is being transmitted over an electronic
communications network.

Implementation Specification:
§164.312(e)(2)(ii) Encryption (Addressable) — Implement a mechanism to encrypt
electronic protected health information whenever deemed appropriate.

Guidance from HHS Office for Civil Rights:
While encryption is listed as "addressable" rather than "required," a covered entity
must assess whether encryption is a reasonable and appropriate safeguard in its
environment. If the entity decides that encryption is not reasonable and appropriate,
the entity must document that determination and implement an equivalent alternative
measure. In practice, encryption of ePHI at rest and in transit is considered the
de facto standard for HIPAA compliance. The vast majority of risk assessments conclude
that encryption is reasonable and appropriate given the current threat landscape.

For AWS workloads containing ePHI:
- S3 buckets must use server-side encryption (SSE-KMS recommended for audit trail)
- RDS instances must enable encryption at rest and enforce SSL/TLS for connections
- EBS volumes must be encrypted
- Data in transit must use TLS 1.2 or higher
- SSE-KMS is preferred over SSE-S3 because KMS provides CloudTrail audit logs of
  every key usage event, which is critical evidence for HIPAA compliance audits.
