HIPAA Security Rule — Access Control | 45 CFR §164.312(a)(1)

Covered entities must implement technical policies and procedures that allow only authorised persons or software programs to access electronic protected health information (ePHI). This standard requires a unique user identification mechanism so every access event can be traced to a specific individual. Emergency access procedures must also exist so authorised personnel can obtain ePHI during a crisis without bypassing accountability. Automatic logoff is an addressable implementation specification — it must be implemented or a documented reason for non-implementation must exist. Encryption and decryption of ePHI at rest is also an addressable specification under this standard; for organisations with high risk tolerance or immature compliance programmes, treating it as required is the defensible choice. Failure to enforce access control is one of the most commonly cited HIPAA violations and frequently results in HHS Office for Civil Rights penalties exceeding $1 million per incident category.
